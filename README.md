## 📌 Project Overview

This project is a **professional Phishing Awareness Training Module** developed as part of the **CodeAlpha Cybersecurity Internship (Task 2)**. It is designed as a complete training deck that organizations, educators, and individuals can use to raise awareness about phishing attacks — one of the most dangerous and prevalent cyber threats today.

> 🚨 **Did you know?** Over **3.4 billion phishing emails** are sent every single day. This training module helps people recognize, resist, and report them.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- ✅ Explain what phishing is and its different types
- ✅ Teach users how to recognize phishing emails and fake websites
- ✅ Educate about social engineering tactics used by attackers
- ✅ Provide actionable best practices and safety tips
- ✅ Include real-world breach examples for context
- ✅ Test knowledge with interactive quiz questions

## 🌍 Real-World Cases Referenced

### 🔴 DNC Spear Phishing Attack (2016)
A targeted spear phishing email tricked a campaign chairman into revealing his Gmail password, leading to one of the most politically significant data breaches in history.
**Lesson:** Two-factor authentication could have prevented this entirely.

### 🟠 Twitter/X Social Engineering Breach (2020)
Attackers called Twitter employees posing as IT support, gaining access to internal admin tools. 130 high-profile accounts were hijacked and $120K in crypto was stolen.
**Lesson:** Always verify caller identity through official channels.

### 🔵 Uber MFA Fatigue Attack (2022)
An attacker bombarded an employee with MFA prompts until they accidentally approved one, granting full internal network access.
**Lesson:** Never approve MFA prompts you didn't initiate — report them immediately.

## ✅ The 5 Golden Rules

```
🛑  STOP   — Don't react immediately to urgent requests
🤔  THINK  — Ask: Is this expected? Does it make sense?
🔍  CHECK  — Verify sender, URL, and content before acting
✅  VERIFY — Confirm via another channel if still unsure
🚨  REPORT — Alert your IT/security team about anything suspicious
```

## 📚 Learning Outcomes

After completing this training, participants will be able to:

- Identify the different types of phishing attacks
- Spot red flags in suspicious emails (fake domains, urgency, generic greetings)
- Distinguish legitimate URLs from malicious look-alike domains
- Understand the psychology behind social engineering
- Apply the STOP-THINK-CHECK-VERIFY-REPORT framework
- Know when and how to report a suspected phishing attempt
